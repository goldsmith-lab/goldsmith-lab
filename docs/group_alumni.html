Dr. Jin-Xun Liu

Postdoctoral Researcher (2017–2019)

View C.V.

Dr. Jin-Xun Liu received his Ph.D in theoretical catalysis from Dalian Institute of Chemical Physics, Chinese Academy of Sciences (2015) under the supervision of Prof. Wei-Xue Li. After, he worked as a postdoctoral researcher in the Department of Chemical Engineering & Chemistry at Eindhoven University of Technology with Prof. Emiel J.M. Hensen (2015-2018). His current research interests are focused on computational catalysis including: structure sensitivity of nanocatalysts, reaction mechanisms, nanoscale effects in heterogeneous catalysis and electrocatalysis, molecular simulation, and machine learning.

Last known location or occupation: Faculty member at University of Science and Technology of China

Email: jinxun@umich.ede

Art blog

Rucha Railkar, completed her Bachelor’s in Chemical Engineering at Mumbai University, India and her Master's in chemical engineering at Carnegie Mellon University in December 2020, with a research project under Prof. Andrew Gellman. She worked with Prof. Mittal at Lehigh University as a visiting research assistant during Spring 2021. She now works with Prof. Goldsmith as a summer visiting research and will begin her PhD program at the University of Delaware in Fall 2021. Her interests lie in modeling and optimizing catalytic systems by using ab initio methods, optimization techniques, data science, and more. Rucha runs her canvas painting shop on Etsy and also a blog, “The_Kalakruti”. Apart from this, she strums the Ukulele and sings to it, cooks, socializes and pets animals.

Alex King

Undergrad Researcher (2019–2020)

Alex is a senior undergraduate student studying Chemical Engineering and minoring in Chemistry. His previous research at UMich has involved experimental work in heterogeneous catalysis for converting methane into higher-valued chemicals. Additionally, Alex has participated in a summer Research Experiences for Undergraduates (REU) program at the University of Delaware studying hydroxide exchange membrane electrolyzers (2018) and in the Amgen Scholars program at the University of California, Berkeley investigating a common surface contaminant in lithium-ion batteries (2019). To explore computation, he has joined the Goldsmith Lab in Fall 2019 to model the coverage of poisonous species on nitrate reduction catalysts. Other than research, Alex enjoys anything Frisbee-related, cooking, hiking, basketball, and exercising.
